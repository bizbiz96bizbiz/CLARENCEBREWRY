## Deep Analysis: Top 5

**Most relevant** — also a brewery conversion, also in Australia.
- Architectural pedigree front and centre (Tonkin Zulaikha Greer design firm credited)
- High ceilings, original windows, timber paneling used as hero imagery
- Multi-venue storytelling (3 restaurants, bar, rooftop pool)
- Design publications featured prominently (badges + links)
- CTA: "Explore" → "Stay" → specific room types

### 2. Capella Sydney (9.5/10)
**Design benchmark** — sets the ceiling for what heritage accommodation sites look like.
- Cinematic full-screen video and photography
- Museum-quality artwork curation featured in copy
- Architectural photography by specialists
- Social proof via Wallpaper, est living, architecture awards
- Understated CTAs ("Discover" not "Book Now")

### 3. Greyleigh (8.5/10)
**Tone benchmark** — similar intimate, design-forward B&B scale.
- Bold, opinionated color palette used throughout (differentiation from generic beige)
- Interior design narrative prominent on every page
- Designer credits (Duet, Studio Rewild, Emily Simpson)
- Full-estate experience positioning
- Social proof: design press features, not just guest reviews

### 4. The Sir George (8.5/10)
**Scale benchmark** — similar intimate rural NSW setting.
- Strong drovers/coaches history narrative woven throughout
- High-quality lifestyle photography — not just room shots
- "Heritage Stay" directories used (Historic Stays listing)

### 5. Mona Farm (8/10)
**Experience benchmark** — multi-building estate approach.
- Multiple heritage buildings presented as a "collection"
- Contemporary art + sculpture integrated with heritage
- Events/weddings as secondary revenue stream
- Luxury Travel Mag and Booking.com social proof

## Patterns of the Top 10%

### Pattern 1: Narrative Layering
Every page has a story arc — building history → restoration journey → current experience → your stay. Budget sites just list amenities. Elite sites make you feel the timeline.

### Pattern 2: Cinematic Photography Standards
Museum-quality architectural photography. Consistent warm colour grading. Tactile detail shots (timber grain, stone texture, cast-iron). Video used in hero sections. Budget sites use phone photos.

### Pattern 3: Heritage-Specific CTAs
"Experience the Brewery" / "Reserve the Heritage Suite" / "Plan Your Stay" — not generic "Book Now." Every CTA ties back to a unique property feature.

### Pattern 4: Design Credits & Restoration Story
The best sites celebrate the architect, the restoration team, the design choices. This validates premium positioning. Visitors feel they're buying into a curated work, not just renting a room.

### Pattern 5: Third-Party Validation via Design Press
Not just TripAdvisor stars — features in Wallpaper, est living, Architecture Australia, Luxury Travel Mag. These logos on the homepage signal "this place has been curated and recognised." Budget sites only show booking platform widgets.
